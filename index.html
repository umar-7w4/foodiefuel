Chaga Mushroom.

Chaga mushroom, also known as Inonotus obliquus, has been used for medicinal purposes for centuries. It is rich in beta-glucans, a type of polysaccharide that boosts the immune system by stimulating immune cells such as macrophages, natural killer cells, and neutrophils to recognize and destroy harmful pathogens effectively.

This mushroom also contains antioxidants, including superoxide dismutase (SOD) and melanin, which protect cells from oxidative stress caused by free radicals. Oxidative stress can damage cells, including immune cells, and weaken the immune system, making the body more vulnerable to infections and diseases.

Research has demonstrated that chaga mushroom can help regulate cytokine production, which are proteins that function as chemical messengers in the immune system. When the immune system detects a pathogen, cytokines signal immune cells to attack. An overproduction of cytokines can lead to chronic inflammation and autoimmune disorders. Chaga mushroom helps to regulate cytokine production, preventing excessive inflammation and promoting a healthy immune response.
